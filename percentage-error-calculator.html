How to Calculate Percent Error in Experiments

Percentage error measures how close your experimental results are to the accepted value. This tool from Percentage Calculators is essential for lab reports.

Percent Error Formula

Percent Error = (|Experimental - Theoretical| ÷ |Theoretical|) × 100

Example: Density Measurement

Measured aluminum density: 2.85 g/cm³. Accepted: 2.70 g/cm³. Error: (|2.85-2.70| ÷ 2.70) × 100 = 5.56%.

Error Interpretation

  • < 5%: Good accuracy for most labs
  • 5-10%: Moderate accuracy
  • > 10%: High error, investigate procedure
